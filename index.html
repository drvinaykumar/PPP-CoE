§ 01 — The Architecture

One Centre. Many themes.

The PPP Centre rests on a deliberate governance philosophy. Intellectual vitality at the boundaries of disciplines is best sustained not by rigid silos, but by a federal model — shared sovereignty at the level of the Centre, meaningful autonomy at the level of each theme.

Every funded project at PPP carries a non-negotiable condition: the meaningful participation of undergraduate students in the research process. This is not a peripheral criterion — it is a core one, ensuring that the boundary between research and teaching remains permeable, which is precisely the boundary that defines a liberal arts research culture.

As themes mature — in faculty, funding, publications, and external partnerships — they are designed to evolve into fully independent units under the PPP umbrella. The Centre is not a fixed structure but a living one: an incubator for the next generation of TSLAS’s intellectual infrastructure.

The model is deliberately analogous to a federal state: shared sovereignty at the level of the Centre, meaningful autonomy at the level of the theme.

— TSLAS Strategy 2030

§ 02 — The Eight Themes

Where the work lives.

Eight domains in which TSLAS faculty have built scholarly momentum and where the liberal arts have a distinctive analytical contribution to make.

01 Policy

Higher Education Policy & Governance

Evidence-based research informing policy and institutional transformation for the universities of the future — locally and globally. TSLAS as a site of scholarly reflection on the very conditions that govern its own existence.

? Anchored by the Thapar Higher Education Survey (THES) — a longitudinal four-year cycle.

02 Pedagogy

Pedagogy & Curricular Innovation

Designing and testing new models of teaching, assessment, and engagement aligned with the realities of the digital, AI-driven era — with a particular focus on soft pedagogical interventions.

? Subtle, low-cost shifts in questioning, framing, sequencing, feedback, and relational tone — that profoundly reshape learning.

03 Science

Science of Learning

Understanding how humans learn — from neurons to networks — and how technology, motivation, and context shape the learning process. Bridging cognitive psychology, neuroscience, and educational practice.

? How does chronic AI co-pilot use affect long-term memory and executive function?

04 Sustainability

Sustainability & Human Flourishing

How can higher education nurture a generation of learners who see sustainability not as sacrifice, but as a joyful expression of human flourishing and excellence-seeking?

? Three pillars: ecosystem restoration, renewable energy, and circular economy — under a 6E framework.

05 Mind & Culture

Mind and Culture Studies

How personality and culture interact to shape thought, behaviour, and institutions — studied on their own conceptual terms rather than through Western templates.

? Towards an Indigenous Culture Inventory for India and South Asia: Excellence Orientation, Micro-Collectivism, Binary Orientation.

06 Neuro

Neuro-Management

The application of neuroscience and cognitive science to leadership, creativity, decision-making, and organisational behaviour — through experimental methods and data-driven analysis.

? EEG and eye-tracking studies on cognitive load, team synchronisation, and consumer attention.

07 IKS

Indian Knowledge System

How can Indian Knowledge Systems contribute to a contemporary global philosophy of education, sustainability, and human flourishing — without losing epistemic integrity or cultural depth?

? Rigorously empirical: reclaiming indigenous epistemologies through comparative and quantitative scholarship.

08 AI

AI for Education

How artificial intelligence is reshaping teaching, learning, and institutional design across India’s higher education landscape — with a central focus on ethical deployment.

? The scholarly infrastructure behind TSLAS’s DISHA Model and AI Readiness Framework.

§ 04 — Sub Units

Themes becoming centres.

Two of the PPP themes have already achieved enough institutional weight to operate as sub-units, with their own scholarly identities and external profiles.

Sub‑unit

ACT

Art, Cognition & Technology Centre

ACT applies neuroscience and cognitive science to leadership, creativity, and decision-making. Through experimental methods and data-driven analysis, it develops new frameworks for understanding human cognition in technological and managerial contexts. Connected directly to TSLAS’s eye-tracking laboratory and to the ambition of building a full Cognitive and Neuroscience lab within this strategy period.

Trajectory: from PPP sub-unit toward fully independent Centre of Excellence.

§ 05 — The DISHA Connection

The PPP Centre is the scholarly home of DISHA.

The Pedagogy & Curricular Innovation and AI for Education themes form the institutional home of DISHA — the India-centred educational philosophy at the heart of TSLAS’s Strategy 2030. Faculty here do not merely implement the model; they study, evaluate, and publish on it — ensuring the model improves over time through evidence rather than assumption.
